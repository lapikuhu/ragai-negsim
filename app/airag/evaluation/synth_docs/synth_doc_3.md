# Project Delivery Reference A

## 1. critical path

The critical path is the longest chain of dependent project tasks and determines the earliest possible completion date.

## 2. scope creep

Scope creep is the gradual addition of unapproved work without matching adjustments to time, cost, or resources.

## 3. risk register

A risk register records identified threats, their likelihood and impact, owners, and planned responses.

## 4. retrospective

A retrospective is a recurring team meeting used to inspect recent work and choose process improvements.

## 5. work in progress limit

A work-in-progress limit caps simultaneous tasks so a team finishes work before starting more.

## 6. dependency

A dependency exists when one task cannot start or finish until another task reaches a required state.

## 7. milestone

A milestone marks a significant project event or checkpoint and has no duration of its own.

## 8. stakeholder

A stakeholder is a person or group that affects, is affected by, or perceives itself affected by a project.

## 9. baseline

A project baseline is the approved scope, schedule, or cost plan used as the reference for measuring change.

## 10. change control

Change control is the formal process for proposing, assessing, approving, and recording project changes.
