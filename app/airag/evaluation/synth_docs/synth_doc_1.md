# Negotiation Reference A

## 1. BATNA

A negotiator's BATNA is the best realistic option available if the current negotiation ends without agreement.

## 2. reservation point

A reservation point is the least favorable deal a negotiator is willing to accept before walking away.

## 3. target point

A target point is the realistic outcome a negotiator plans to pursue through offers and concessions.

## 4. ZOPA

The zone of possible agreement is the overlap between the terms each side can accept.

## 5. anchoring

Anchoring occurs when an initial serious proposal influences the range of later discussion.

## 6. interests

Interests are the underlying needs and concerns that explain why a party takes a stated position.

## 7. integrative bargaining

Integrative bargaining creates value by trading across issues that the parties value differently.

## 8. objective criteria

Objective criteria are independent standards, such as market data or precedent, used to evaluate proposals.

## 9. concession

A concession is a movement from a prior demand, ideally exchanged for movement by the other side.

## 10. caucus

A caucus is a private break in which one side or a mediator can reassess options away from the joint table.
