# Negotiation Reference B

## 1. deadline

A deadline can accelerate decisions, but an artificial deadline may damage trust if exposed.

## 2. package offer

A package offer presents several connected terms together so trade-offs can be assessed as a whole.

## 3. contingent agreement

A contingent agreement ties future obligations to an uncertain event that the parties predict differently.

## 4. strategic silence

Strategic silence gives the other side room to respond and can prevent unnecessary self-concessions.

## 5. open question

An open question invites explanation and cannot usually be answered with only yes or no.

## 6. summary

A negotiation summary checks shared understanding by restating agreed points and unresolved issues.

## 7. authority

Decision authority is the formal power a representative has to commit their organization to a deal.

## 8. logrolling

Logrolling exchanges concessions on issues each side values differently, allowing mutual gains.

## 9. MESO

Multiple equivalent simultaneous offers reveal preferences by presenting several packages of equal value to the sender.

## 10. impasse

An impasse is a point where talks stall because no currently proposed option is acceptable to both sides.
